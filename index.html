What is The Econ Lab?

It's a coded tool created to help users understand the economic effects of changes in our environment. It compounds net effects from various environmental factors and shows how they ripple through the economy: government spending, prices, livelihoods, and growth.

By fetching real life LIVE data from official sources like Open-Meteo, World Bank Open Data, or GBIF, it provides verifiable and reliable results. Other figures marked with "Verified June 2026" in their source line are real published numbers from government and industrial sources. Every figure across all five dashboards is now either auto-updating or a verified real number, none are placeholder estimates.

Each section includes an ex ante prediction, which analyzes historical patterns and current data to provide a concise, evidence based projection of potential future outcomes. These predictions are intended to illustrate likely scenarios rather than definitive forecasts.

The Econ Lab Monthly is a source for monthly summaries across all five sections. It also states recent policies and acts with respect to the environment.
